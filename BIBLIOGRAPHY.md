## Anthropology & Thick Description

### Common Sense as a Cultural System
> Geertz, C. (1983). Common Sense as a Cultural System. In *Local Knowledge: Further Essays in Interpretive Anthropology* (pp. 73–93). Basic Books.

### Culture Re-Engineers Man
> Geertz, C. (1973). The Impact of the Concept of Culture on the Concept of Man. In *The Interpretation of Cultures: Selected Essays* (pp. 33–54). Basic Books.

### Play, Rules & Serious Muddles
> Geertz, C. (1973). Deep Play: Notes on the Balinese Cockfight. In *The Interpretation of Cultures: Selected Essays* (pp. 412–453). Basic Books.

> Bateson, G. (1972). A Theory of Play and Fantasy. In *Steps to an Ecology of Mind* (pp. 177–193). Ballantine Books. (Originally published 1955)

**Key concept**: Thick description—recording not just behavior but the layered meanings that make behavior intelligible within a cultural context. Play frames and metacommunication.

## Cybernetics & Systems Theory

### Dolphin Talk: Code of Relationship
> Bateson, G. (1972). Problems in Cetacean and Other Mammalian Communication. In *Steps to an Ecology of Mind* (pp. 364–378). Ballantine Books.

### Grace, Style & Info in Art
> Bateson, G. (1972). Style, Grace, and Information in Primitive Art. In *Steps to an Ecology of Mind* (pp. 128–152). Ballantine Books.

### What the Frog's Eye Tells the Brain
> Lettvin, J.Y., Maturana, H.R., McCulloch, W.S., & Pitts, W.H. (1959). What the Frog's Eye Tells the Frog's Brain. *Proceedings of the IRE*, 47(11), 1940–1951.

### Darwin Among the Machines
> Butler, S. (1863, June 13). Darwin Among the Machines. *The Press* (Christchurch, New Zealand).

### Cybernetics & Ghosts
> Calvino, I. (1986). Cybernetics and Ghosts. In P. Creagh (Trans.), *The Uses of Literature* (pp. 3–27). Harcourt Brace Jovanovich. (Lecture originally delivered 1967)

**Key concept**: Feedback loops—how systems regulate themselves through circular causation and information exchange.

## Media Theory

### Heidegger: Questioning Tech's Essence
> Heidegger, M. (1977). The Question Concerning Technology. In W. Lovitt (Trans.), *The Question Concerning Technology and Other Essays* (pp. 3–35). Harper & Row. (Originally delivered as lecture in 1954)

### Turing's Test: Can Machines Think?
> Turing, A.M. (1950). Computing Machinery and Intelligence. *Mind*, 59(236), 433–460.

**See also** (for McLuhan context):
> McLuhan, M. (1964). *Understanding Media: The Extensions of Man*. McGraw-Hill.

**Key concept**: Hot and cool media—high-definition vs. low-definition channels requiring different levels of audience participation.

## Semiotics & Communication

### Wrestling as Mythic Spectacle
> Barthes, R. (1972). The World of Wrestling. In A. Lavers (Trans.), *Mythologies* (pp. 15–25). Hill and Wang. (Originally published 1957)

### Noise: Between Chaos & Death
> Serres, M. (1982). *The Parasite* (L.R. Schehr, Trans.). Johns Hopkins University Press. (Originally published 1980)

### Satyrs' Meals: Host, Guest, Parasite
> Serres, M. (1982). *The Parasite* (L.R. Schehr, Trans.). Johns Hopkins University Press.

### Operative Ekphrasis in AI
> Bajohr, H. (2024). Operative Ekphrasis: The Collapse of the Image-Text Distinction in AI Image Generators. *Word & Image*.

**Key concept**: The parasite—noise as a third party that interrupts, transforms, and enables communication.

## Philosophy of Language

### Augustine to Language-Games
> Wittgenstein, L. (1953). *Philosophical Investigations* (G.E.M. Anscombe, Trans.). Blackwell. (See opening sections on Augustine's picture of language)

### Wittgenstein's Model-Milieu 1914
> Wittgenstein, L. (1922). *Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus* (C.K. Ogden, Trans.). Routledge & Kegan Paul.

### Language as Concept Translation
> Jakobson, R. (1959). On Linguistic Aspects of Translation. In R.A. Brower (Ed.), *On Translation* (pp. 232–239). Harvard University Press.

**Key concept**: Language-games—meaning as use within forms of life, not as picture-correspondence.
