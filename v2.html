Metis vs Thin Plans
Match M12 — interpretive card
Verdict: Light favours thick, negotiated design.
Metis: thick, adaptive plans
  • Plans as promises to negotiate with materials, not commands to override them.
  • Leans on examples, metaphors, and ritual phrasing as thickening agents for AI prompts.
  • Treats every mis-build as a diagnostic: where did language, world, and agent stop agreeing?
Thin Plans: brittle, schematic control
  • Imagines the world as inert substrate for instructions rather than a recalcitrant partner.
  • Favors single-shot, one-way commands over multi-turn, context-thickening dialogue.
  • Collapses when the AI’s errors are treated as bugs to erase, not signals to reinterpret.

Read this matchup as a design diagnostic: if your interface feels like Thin Plans, add thickening agents — roles, metaphors, examples, and negotiated steps — until the AI behaves more like Metis in the field than a spreadsheet in the sky.
