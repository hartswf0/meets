Thick Prompts: A Cybernetic Approach to AI Viscosity

W.F. Harts · 2024 · MEETS Archive

Abstract

Contemporary AI interfaces prioritize "frictionless" interaction, reducing human intent to thin, transactional queries. This practice-based dissertation argues that meaningful human-AI collaboration requires viscosity—a deliberate thickening of the communicative medium.

By synthesizing Geertzian thick description with Batesonian cybernetics, I propose a framework where "thick prompts" act as high-viscosity feedback loops. Unlike efficient commands, these prompts resist immediate closure, slowing the system's collapse into generic probability and opening space for emergent, context-rich meaning.

Keywords: thick description, cybernetics, semiotic viscosity, prompt engineering, human-AI collaboration, worlding
ENTER ARCHIVE

Central Argument

Prompt engineering is a misnomer; the challenge is not to engineer efficient commands but to cultivate semiotic viscosity. The generative capacity of an AI system is directly proportional to the cultural and contextual "thickness" of its input, transforming the prompt from a directive into a shared, cybernetic environment.

  1. From Transaction to Environment Thin prompts treat AI as a tool for retrieval; thick prompts treat AI as an environment for thought. By embedding cultural density, we shift from extracting answers to inhabiting shared semiotic worlds.
  2. The Viscosity Principle Resistance is necessary for traction. Just as physical movement requires friction, intellectual movement requires semiotic resistance. Viscous prompts provide the necessary "drag" to prevent the model from sliding into hallucination or banality.
  3. Cybernetic Worlding Prompting is a recursive act of world-building ("worlding"). Following McLuhan, the medium of the prompt shapes the message of the output. A "cool," low-definition prompt demands high participation, while a "hot," thick prompt extends the user's cognitive model into the machine.
